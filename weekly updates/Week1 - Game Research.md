# 2. Game-by-Game Stealth Design Analysis

## 2.1 The Cleaner

**Scope & Accessibility**
- Small-scale game.
- Free and simple to operate.
- Designed for beginners.

**Pacing & Pressure**
- Chase sequence appears only in the final level.
- Low pressure for most of the game.

**Research Value**
- Suitable reference for onboarding and beginner-friendly stealth pacing.

---

## 2.2 Serial Cleaners

**Perspective & Camera**
- Top-down third-person stealth game.

**Level Design**
- Features diverse level terrain.
- Terrain and layout design are worth referencing.

**Overall Evaluation**
- Solid stealth game.
- Not particularly outstanding.

---

## 2.3 Invisible, Inc.

**Developer & Genre**
- Developed by Klei Entertainment.
- Niche title with roguelike elements.

**Core Stealth Mechanics**
- Avoiding enemy line of sight using terrain cover.
- Enemies have fixed patrol routes and pursuit routes.
- Includes sound-based alert system.
- Players can throw items to distract or lure enemies.

**Gameplay Structure**
- Turn-based stealth.
- All levels are randomly generated.
- Wide selection of playable characters, skills, and items.

**Strategic Depth**
- Late-game gameplay feels strategic and chess-like.
- Requires careful planning.

**Balance Considerations**
- Balance issues exist.
- Late game can become difficult without strong early items.

**Research Value**
- Covers almost all mechanics of top-down stealth games.
- Extremely worthwhile reference.

---

## 2.4 Mark of the Ninja Remastered

**Developer & Presentation**
- Developed by Klei Entertainment.
- Pure 2D stealth game.

**Mechanics & Systems**
- Skill system closely tied to gameplay.
- Map design supports stealth mechanics.

**Overall Evaluation**
- Solid stealth game.
- Not outstanding, but useful as a reference.

---

## 2.5 Thief Series

**Franchise Context**
- Developed by Eidos-Montréal.
- Considered the godfather of 3D stealth games.

**Version Recommendation**
- First three titles are very old (over 20 years) and not recommended.
- Thief (2014) is recommended.

**World & Level Design**
- Semi-open world structure.
- Hub-based main map resembling real-world environments.
- Extremely high degree of freedom.

**Stealth Interaction**
- Multiple infiltration approaches per location.
- Players can:
  - Sneak past enemies
  - Destroy environmental light sources (e.g., oil lamps)
  - Directly eliminate enemies

**Research Value**
- Strong reference for map design and environmental interaction.

---

## 2.6 Dishonored Series

**Design Foundation**
- Shares core strengths with the Thief series.
- Adds superpower mechanics to traditional stealth logic.

**Level Design**
- Considered a milestone in game level design.
- Complex environments with strong navigation guidance.

**Time-Based Mechanics**
- Players can traverse time within the same location.
- Actions in the past affect future maps and storylines.
- Enables access to new areas and altered narrative outcomes.

**Environmental Systems**
- Mechanized mansions with changeable layouts.
- Highly intricate but well-structured spaces.

**Overall Evaluation**
- Highly impressive.
- Strongly recommended as a design reference.

---

## 2.7 Assassin’s Creed Series

**Franchise Scale**
- Very well-known franchise with many entries.

**Stealth Focus Variability**
- Some titles emphasize stealth.
- Others lean toward hack-and-slash combat.

**Design Limitations**
- Suffers from repetitive, cookie-cutter design in later stages.

**Selection Advice**
- Recommended to research individual entries before choosing.
- Later releases usually have more content but weaker stealth focus.

---

## 2.8 Thief Simulator / Crime Simulator Series

**Core Gameplay Loop**
- Almost no ability to counter NPCs directly.
- Players can only hide or flee based on NPC movement routes.

**Stealth Style**
- Extreme hide-and-seek gameplay.
- Tense but not horror-oriented.

**System Depth**
- Other systems are solid but shallow.
- Lacks rich skills or complex mechanics.

**Overall Evaluation**
- Playable when short on games.

---

## 2.9 Watch Dogs 2

**Developer & Genre**
- Open-world game by Ubisoft.
- Hacker-themed stealth gameplay.

**Stealth Approach**
- Emphasizes hacking over physical infiltration.
- Players can:
  - Remotely control environmental objects to eliminate NPCs
  - Hack networks to disable cameras and security systems

**Design Characteristics**
- Fun but somewhat cookie-cutter.
- Remote interaction mechanics are worth referencing.

---

## 2.10 Kingdom Come: Deliverance Series

**World & Simulation**
- Large-scale medieval open-world hardcore simulator.
- Extremely realistic and immersive.

**Action Structure**
- All actions must be completed step by step.
- Example: alchemy requires gathering, grinding, mixing, distilling, and bottling.

**Stealth Role**
- Stealth gameplay exists but is not the main focus.
- Entire game can be played through stealth (mainly theft).

**Player Freedom**
- Very high degree of freedom.
- Extremely large in scale.

**Audience Consideration**
- May frustrate impatient players.

---

## 2.11 Payday 2

**Play Mode**
- Requires online co-op.

**Gameplay Balance**
- Supports both stealth and brute-force approaches.
- Focuses more on teamwork and shooting than pure stealth evasion.

---

## 2.12 Limbo / Inside

**Presentation**
- Small-scale 2.5D side-scrolling games.

**Gameplay Focus**
- Core gameplay is puzzle-solving.
- Includes some stealth elements.

**Scope**
- Around 5 hours to complete each.

---
